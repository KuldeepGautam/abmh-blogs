What is MICS?

Minimally Invasive Cardiac Surgery is a specialized technique where cardiac procedures are performed through small incisions typically between 2 to 4 inches without opening the entire chest. This approach allows surgeons to reach the heart through intercostal spaces using high-precision instruments.

How It Differs from Traditional Open-Heart Surgery

Unlike traditional surgeries that require a full sternotomy (6–10 inch incision), MICS uses small, targeted openings. This results in:

  • Reduced blood loss
  • Less post-operative pain
  • Minimal scarring
