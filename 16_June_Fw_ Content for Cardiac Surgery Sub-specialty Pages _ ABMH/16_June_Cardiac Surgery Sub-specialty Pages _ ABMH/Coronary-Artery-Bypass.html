Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG) Surgery in Pimpri, Pune

Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting, commonly known as CABG, is a surgical procedure aimed at restoring blood flow to the heart muscle. It involves bypassing blocked or narrowed coronary arteries using grafts taken from other parts of the body, such as veins from the leg or arteries from the chest or wrist. This surgery plays a crucial role in preventing heart attacks and improving the quality of life for patients with significant coronary artery disease.

When is CABG Recommended?

  • Blocked or narrowed coronary arteries
  • Severe chest pain (angina) due to coronary artery disease (CAD)
  • Multiple diseased coronary arteries
  • Failed angioplasty or stent placement
  • Left main coronary artery disease

Symptoms Indicating the Need for CABG

  • Persistent chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue with minimal exertion
  • Irregular heartbeats or palpitations

CABG Surgery Procedure

  • Preoperative assessment and diagnosis
  • Types of grafts used: vein from the leg, artery from the chest/wrist
  • Surgical steps (with/without heart-lung machine)
  • Minimally invasive options

Types of Coronary Artery Bypass Surgeries

  • Traditional CABG
  • Off-pump CABG
  • Minimally Invasive Direct Coronary Artery Bypass (MIDCAB)
  • Robotic-assisted CABG

Risk Factors & Complications

  • Possible surgical risks
  • Postoperative complications (bleeding, arrhythmia, infection)
  • Factors that increase risk: age, diabetes, kidney disease

Frequently Asked Questions about Heart Bypass Surgery (CABG)

1. Why is coronary bypass surgery done?

To restore blood flow to the heart when coronary arteries are blocked or narrowed.

2. Is coronary artery bypass graft a major surgery?

Yes, it is a significant open-heart procedure but commonly performed with high success.

3. What is grafting in bypass surgery?

Grafting involves using blood vessels from other body parts to bypass blocked coronary arteries.

4. How long is a coronary artery bypass graft surgery?

Typically, the surgery lasts 3 to 6 hours depending on complexity.

5. Is coronary artery bypass graft surgery safe?

When performed by experienced surgeons with proper care, it is generally safe.

6. What is the recovery time?

Full recovery may take 6 to 12 weeks with adherence to rehabilitation.

7. Who is a candidate for CABG?

Patients with severe coronary artery disease not suitable for angioplasty or stenting.

8. Is open heart and bypass surgery the same?

CABG is a type of open-heart surgery involving grafting arteries or veins to improve blood flow.
