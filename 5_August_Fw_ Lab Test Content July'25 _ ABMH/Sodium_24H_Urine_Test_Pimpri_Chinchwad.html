What is the Sodium, 24-Hour Urine Test?

This test measures the amount of sodium (salt) your body passes out through urine in a 24-hour period. Sodium is important for fluid balance, nerve function, and muscle activity. The test is useful for detecting how well your kidneys regulate sodium, especially if you’re dealing with blood pressure problems or signs of dehydration. Why is this Test Done?

Doctors recommend this test to:

  • Evaluate kidney function and how sodium is processed
  • Help diagnose conditions like dehydration, kidney disease, or hormonal imbalances
  • Monitor salt levels in patients with high or low blood pressure
  • Assess treatment effectiveness for certain chronic illnesses

Who Should Get This Test Done?

This test is usually recommended if you experience:

  • Fluctuating blood pressure
  • Unexplained swelling (edema)
  • Signs of dehydration
  • Kidney or heart-related conditions
  • Frequent urination or fatigue

When to Consult a Doctor After the Test?

Speak to your doctor if:

  • Your sodium levels are too high or too low
  • You experience persistent symptoms such as weakness, dizziness, or leg swelling
  • Results are outside the normal reference range and require medical attention
