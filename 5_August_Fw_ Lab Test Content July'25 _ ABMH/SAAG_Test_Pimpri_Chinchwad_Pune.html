What is the Serum Ascites Albumin Gradient (SAAG) Test?

The SAAG Test helps identify the reason behind fluid buildup in your abdomen (known as ascites). It works by comparing albumin protein levels in your blood and in the fluid from your abdomen. This test is especially useful for checking if the cause is related to liver issues like cirrhosis or something else like infection or cancer. Why is this Test Done?

Doctors recommend the Albumin Gradient Test to:

  • Find the cause of ascites (abdominal fluid collection)
  • Check for liver-related issues like portal hypertension
  • Differentiate between liver disease and other conditions like cancer or infections
  • Monitor liver cirrhosis or heart-related complications

Parameters Measured

  • Serum albumin level
  • Ascitic fluid albumin level
  • SAAG value (difference between serum and ascitic albumin)

Preparation for the Test

  • No fasting is required
  • Drink water and eat normally unless advised otherwise by your doctor
  • Ascitic fluid may need to be collected in a clinical setting

Who Should Get This Test Done?

You may need this test if you have:

  • Persistent or unexplained abdominal swelling
  • Liver disease like cirrhosis
  • Kidney or heart conditions that can cause fluid retention
  • Symptoms suggesting abdominal infections or cancer

When to Consult a Doctor After the Test?

  • Your SAAG value is above 1.1 g/dL (may indicate liver-related cause)
  • Your value is below 1.1 g/dL (may indicate cancer, infection, or inflammation)
  • Symptoms persist or worsen even after testing

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is the normal SAAG value?

A value of 1.1 g/dL or higher usually suggests portal hypertension. Lower values may point to infections or malignancy.

2. Can this test be done at home?

The blood sample may be collected at home. The ascitic fluid sample usually requires a clinical setting.

3. What happens if my SAAG result is abnormal?

You’ll need to consult your doctor for further evaluation.

4. Is there any risk in this test?

The blood test has minimal risks. Ascitic fluid collection may cause mild discomfort but is usually safe when done by a professional.
