What is the Calcium – Spot Urine Test?

The Calcium – Spot Urine Test measures the amount of calcium in a small, single urine sample. It's a quick and simple way to assess how much calcium is being excreted by your body. If you're dealing with frequent kidney stones, bone pain, or related issues, this test can help detect any abnormalities in calcium levels.

Why is this Test Done?

Doctors recommend this test to:

  • Check for high levels of calcium in urine (hypercalciuria)
  • Monitor conditions that affect bones or kidneys
  • Detect risk factors for kidney stones
  • Evaluate parathyroid gland function
  • Assist in the diagnosis of calcium metabolism disorders

Parameters Measured

  • Calcium in urine
  • Sometimes paired with creatinine to assess the calcium/creatinine ratio

This helps in evaluating how much calcium is lost through urine, which is critical in bone health and kidney function assessments.

Preparation for the Test

Who Should Get This Test Done?

Consider getting the test if you experience:

  • Recurrent kidney stones
  • Bone weakness or fractures
  • Unexplained fatigue or muscle cramps
  • Known or suspected parathyroid gland issues
  • You are on long-term calcium or vitamin D supplements

When to Consult a Doctor After the Test?

Speak to your doctor if:

  • Your calcium levels in urine are unusually high or low
  • You continue to have symptoms like back pain, bone pain, or kidney issues
  • You are managing a chronic condition like hyperparathyroidism or osteoporosis
