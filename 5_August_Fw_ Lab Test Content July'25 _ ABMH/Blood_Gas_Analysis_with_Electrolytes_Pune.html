What is the Blood Gas Analysis with Electrolytes Test?

The Blood Gas Analysis with Electrolytes Test checks how well your lungs are working and whether the levels of key electrolytes like sodium and potassium in your blood are balanced. It also helps monitor your blood's pH (acidity/alkalinity). If you're facing breathing issues or unexplained fatigue, you might be advised to get an Arterial Blood Gas with Electrolytes Test in Pimpri Chinchwad or other nearby areas.

Why is this Test Done?

Doctors suggest this test to:

  • Check for lung conditions such as asthma, COPD, or respiratory infections.
  • Monitor oxygen and carbon dioxide levels in the blood.
  • Diagnose metabolic or kidney-related imbalances.
  • Assess electrolyte disturbances causing fatigue, confusion, or irregular heartbeat.

Parameters Measured

This test typically measures:

  • Blood pH (acid-base balance)
  • Oxygen (O₂) and carbon dioxide (CO₂) levels
  • Bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻)
  • Sodium (Na⁺), Potassium (K⁺), Calcium (Ca²⁺), and Chloride (Cl⁻)
  • Oxygen saturation (O₂ Sat)
  • Lactate and Glucose

Preparation for the Test

  • No special preparation required
  • Inform your doctor about ongoing medications, especially oxygen therapy
  • Drink water and eat normally unless advised otherwise

When to Consult a Doctor After the Test?

Contact your doctor if:

  • Results show abnormal oxygen, CO₂, or pH levels
  • Electrolyte imbalances like high potassium or low sodium are detected
  • Symptoms persist despite normal test values

Prompt consultation can help manage serious conditions early.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is the normal range for a Blood Gas Analysis?

Ranges vary, but typically:

  • pH: 7.35–7.45
  • pCO₂: 35–45 mmHg
  • pO₂: 75–100 mmHg
  • HCO₃⁻: 22–28 mEq/L
  • Sodium: 135–145 mEq/L
  • Potassium: 3.5–5.0 mEq/L

2. Can I take this test from home?

Yes, many labs offer Blood Gas Analysis with Electrolytes Test in PCMC with home sample collection.

3. What does a high CO₂ level mean?

It may indicate hypoventilation, respiratory failure, or chronic lung issues. Consult your doctor for further advice.

4. What do abnormal electrolyte levels indicate?

Low/high sodium, potassium, or calcium levels can affect heart, nerve, and muscle function. Prompt attention is needed.

5. Is the test painful?

The arterial blood draw may cause some discomfort but is quick and safe.
