What is the CSF Glucose Test?

The CSF Glucose Test measures the level of glucose (sugar) in the cerebrospinal fluid (CSF), which surrounds and protects the brain and spinal cord. Glucose is an essential energy source for brain function, and its levels in CSF help diagnose neurological conditions.

Why is the CSF Glucose Test Done?

This test helps in detecting:

  • Infections such as Meningitis or Encephalitis – Low CSF glucose levels may indicate bacterial or fungal infections.
  • Neurological Disorders – Abnormal glucose levels can be linked to conditions like central nervous system (CNS) tumors.
  • Monitoring Medical Conditions – It is used to track diseases affecting the brain and spinal cord.

What Parameters Are Covered?

The test measures the glucose concentration in CSF to compare it with blood glucose levels.

How to Prepare for the Test?

  • Your doctor may advise you to fast for a few hours before the test.
  • Inform your healthcare provider about any medications you are taking.
  • The test is performed through a lumbar puncture (spinal tap), so a consent form may be required.

Yes, since CSF glucose is derived from blood glucose, abnormal blood sugar levels can impact CSF glucose readings.
