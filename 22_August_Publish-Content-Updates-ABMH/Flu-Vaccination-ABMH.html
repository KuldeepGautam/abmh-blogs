Why Get a Flu Vaccination?

Getting a flu shot is more than just preventing fever and body aches. Influenza can lead to complications such as pneumonia, bronchitis, sinus infections, and, in severe cases, hospitalisation.

Key benefits of the flu vaccine include:

  • Reducing the risk of flu-related hospital admissions.
  • Lowering the severity of illness if you do catch the flu.
  • Protecting vulnerable groups like infants, pregnant women, and the elderly.
  • Reducing the overall spread of influenza within the community.

By choosing the influenza vaccine in Pimpri-Chinchwad, you safeguard not just yourself but also those around you.

Who Should Get the Flu Vaccine?

The flu vaccine is recommended for almost everyone above 6 months of age. It is especially important for:

  • Young children and infants.
  • Pregnant women.
  • Senior citizens (65+ years).
  • People with chronic conditions such as diabetes, asthma, hypertension, or heart disease.
  • Healthcare professionals and caregivers.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) on Flu Vaccination

1. Can I get the flu vaccine if I have a cold?

If you have mild cold symptoms, you can usually still receive the vaccine. However, in case of high fever or severe illness, your doctor may recommend waiting until you recover.

2. How long does protection from the flu vaccine last?

The flu vaccine protects for about one year, which is why an annual vaccination is recommended.

3. Are there any side effects?

Most people only experience mild side effects such as soreness at the injection site, low-grade fever, or body aches. These typically resolve within a day or two.

4. How soon does the vaccine start working?

It takes about two weeks after vaccination for your body to build immunity against influenza.
