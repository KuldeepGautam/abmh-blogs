Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) on Infectious Diseases

1. What types of infections are treated by the Infectious Diseases department?

The Department of Infectious Diseases at ABMH treats a wide spectrum of infections, including bacterial, viral, fungal, and parasitic diseases. This includes respiratory infections (like tuberculosis and pneumonia), gastrointestinal infections, urinary tract infections, HIV/AIDS, dengue, malaria, hepatitis and hospital-acquired infections. Specialised care is also provided for immunocompromised patients and those with travel-related infections.

2. When should I consult an infectious disease specialist?

You should consult an infectious disease specialist if you have a persistent fever, unexplained infections, repeated illnesses, or if your infection does not respond to standard treatment. Specialists are also consulted for travel-related infections, infections in patients with chronic illnesses or weakened immunity, and for second opinions on serious or rare infections.

3. What is the role of an infectious disease specialist?

An infectious disease specialist diagnoses, treats, and manages complex infections. They identify the source of infection, prescribe targeted antimicrobial treatments, prevent transmission, and manage complications. They also work closely with other departments to ensure comprehensive care, especially in patients with comorbidities or compromised immunity.

4. How are infectious diseases diagnosed?

Infectious diseases are diagnosed using a combination of medical history, physical examination, and advanced lab tests. These may include blood tests, cultures, imaging (X-ray, CT scan), PCR testing, serology, and biopsies. At ABMH, rapid diagnostics and specialised panels help ensure early and accurate detection.

5. What are common symptoms of infectious diseases?

Common symptoms include:

  • Fever or chills
  • Fatigue or weakness
  • Cough, cold, or shortness of breath
  • Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea
  • Skin rashes or lesions
  • Pain, swelling, or pus at a specific site

If these persist, it’s important to consult a top infectious disease doctor in Pimpri Chinchwad.

6. Do you treat both acute and chronic infections?

Yes, the Infectious Diseases department at ABMH provides expert care for both acute infections (like dengue or typhoid) and chronic infections (such as hepatitis B/C, HIV, and tuberculosis). Each case is treated with a personalised care plan based on severity, resistance profile, and patient history.
