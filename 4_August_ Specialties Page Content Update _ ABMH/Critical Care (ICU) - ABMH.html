Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) on Critical Care Unit

1. What is the Critical Care Unit (CCU)?

The Critical Care Unit (CCU) at Aditya Birla Hospital is a highly specialised area designed to treat patients with life-threatening illnesses or conditions requiring constant monitoring and intensive medical care. Staffed by expert intensivists, nurses, and technicians, the CCU offers 24/7 advanced support using modern equipment and is part of what makes ABMH the best critical care hospital in Pimpri Chinchwad.

2. Who needs to be admitted to the Critical Care Unit?

Patients with severe or unstable medical conditions such as respiratory failure, cardiac arrest, sepsis, trauma, post-surgical complications, or multi-organ dysfunction are typically admitted to the CCU. Anyone requiring close observation, advanced life support, or emergency interventions is cared for in this unit under the supervision of the best critical care doctors in Pimpri Chinchwad.

3. What are the common conditions treated in the CCU?

The Department of Critical Care at ABMH treats:

  • Respiratory distress and failure
  • Heart attacks and arrhythmias
  • Stroke and brain trauma
  • Sepsis and severe infections
  • Acute kidney injury
  • Post-operative complications

These critical conditions are managed with precision in our advanced ICU hospital in Pimpri Chinchwad.

4. What is the difference between the ICU and the CCU

At ABMH, ICU (Intensive Care Unit) and CCU (Critical Care Unit) are often integrated, but technically, ICU covers a broad range of critical illnesses, while CCU may specifically handle cardiac-related critical conditions. However, both units are equipped for high-level care, forming a core part of the best critical care hospital in Pune.

5. Is the Critical Care Unit operational 24/7?

Yes, the Critical Care Unit at Aditya Birla Hospital is fully operational 24/7. Our dedicated team of intensivists, critical care nurses, and paramedics ensures round-the-clock patient monitoring, emergency response, and life-saving interventions at all times.

6. Are emergency procedures like ventilation or dialysis available in the CCU?

Absolutely. ABMH’s CCU is equipped with emergency medical infrastructure including mechanical ventilators, non-invasive ventilation, continuous dialysis (CRRT), bedside ultrasound, and advanced monitoring systems. This ensures timely intervention and continuous support for critically ill patients.
