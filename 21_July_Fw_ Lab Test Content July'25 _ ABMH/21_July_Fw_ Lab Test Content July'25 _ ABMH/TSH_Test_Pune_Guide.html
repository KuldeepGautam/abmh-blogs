What is the Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH) Test?

The Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH) Test is a simple blood test that checks how well your thyroid gland is working. It measures the amount of TSH in your blood, a hormone that helps control the production of other important thyroid hormones (T3 and T4).

Getting a TSH Test in PCMC or Pune can help you understand whether your thyroid is overactive (hyperthyroidism) or underactive (hypothyroidism).

Why is This Test Done?

This Test Includes:

  • To diagnose thyroid disorders like hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism
  • To monitor ongoing treatment for thyroid conditions
  • To evaluate unexplained symptoms such as fatigue, weight changes, or mood swings
  • As a routine check-up, especially if there's a family history of thyroid issues
  • For women planning pregnancy or during pregnancy

Who Should Get This Test?

  • People experiencing symptoms of thyroid imbalance (fatigue, weight gain/loss, hair loss, irregular periods, etc.)
  • Individuals with a family history of thyroid disorders
  • Those already diagnosed with thyroid conditions for regular monitoring
  • Women planning a pregnancy or undergoing fertility treatments
  • Elderly individuals or those with autoimmune conditions

When to Consult a Doctor?

  • High TSH levels may suggest an underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism)
  • Low TSH levels could indicate an overactive thyroid (hyperthyroidism)
  • Always discuss results with your doctor, who may suggest additional tests like T3 and T4 for confirmation
  • Medication may be started or adjusted based on the findings
