What Does This Test Include?

This is a single-parameter test that checks the AST (SGOT) enzyme levels in your blood. It’s often part of a larger liver function test panel, but can also be done individually.

What is the SGOT/AST Test?

The SGOT/AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) test is a simple blood test that helps evaluate the health of your liver, muscles, and heart. AST is an enzyme found in these organs, and when there’s any damage, the enzyme leaks into your bloodstream. By measuring AST levels, doctors can identify potential organ damage early.

Why is this Test Done?

You might be advised to take the AST (Aspartate Transaminase) test in PCMC or Pune if you experience symptoms such as:

  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin or eyes)
  • Nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort
  • Unexplained fatigue or weakness
  • Dark urine or pale stools
  • If you have a history of liver conditions or are on medications affecting the liver

Doctors also use this test to:

  • Monitor ongoing liver disease or damage
  • Check the effectiveness of liver treatment
  • Evaluate heart or muscle-related conditions
  • Monitor side effects from certain medications

Parameters Covered

  • Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST / SGOT) – measured in IU/L

How to Prepare

  • No fasting required if the test is done alone
  • If taken with a full liver panel, you may be advised to fast for 8–12 hours
  • Avoid alcohol at least 24 hours before the test
  • Inform your doctor about any medications you're taking
  • Stay hydrated

Doctors also use this test to:

When to See a Doctor After the Test

If your AST levels are higher than the normal range (typically above 40–50 IU/L), consult your doctor. Elevated levels might indicate:

  • Liver inflammation or injury
  • Muscle disorders
  • Heart damage

Your doctor may recommend additional tests like ALT or a complete liver profile.

1. Do I need to fast before the test?

3. What is a normal AST level?
