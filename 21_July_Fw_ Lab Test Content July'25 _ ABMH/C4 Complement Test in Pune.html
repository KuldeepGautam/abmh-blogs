What Is This Test?

The C4 Complement Test is a simple blood test that measures the level of C4 protein in your blood. C4 is part of your immune system and helps your body fight infections and remove damaged cells.

Why is this test done?

Doctors recommend this test to:

  • Help diagnose autoimmune conditions like lupus or rheumatoid arthritis
  • Identify inflammation or infections
  • Monitor diseases that affect the immune system
  • Support diagnosis of rare immune conditions like hereditary angioedema

If you're experiencing unexplained fatigue, joint pain, rashes, or frequent infections, your doctor may suggest this test.

Parameters Covered

This test measures the level of Complement Component 4 (C4) in your blood. It may be ordered alone or alongside a C3 complement test to assess the full complement system.

How to Prepare

  • Fasting: Not strictly required, but some labs may recommend 8–10 hours of fasting
  • Medications: Inform the lab or doctor about any medications or supplements
  • Hydration: Drink water to make blood collection easier

Who Should Get This Test?

  • Have symptoms of autoimmune diseases
  • Have a family history of immune system issues
  • Are being monitored for immune or inflammatory disorders
  • Experience frequent infections or unexplained inflammation

When to Consult a Doctor After the Test

  • Low C4 levels may indicate autoimmune conditions like lupus, chronic hepatitis, or inherited immune deficiencies
  • High C4 levels could be a sign of inflammation, infection, or even certain cancers

Always follow up with a doctor to interpret the results and decide if more tests are needed.
