Frequently Ask Questions (FAQs) on the Robotic Surgery Department

What is robotic-assisted surgery, and how does it work?

Robotic-assisted surgery is a minimally invasive technique where surgeons use robotic systems like the Da Vinci X to perform complex procedures with enhanced precision and control. The system provides a high-definition 3D view and robotic arms that replicate the surgeon’s hand movements with accuracy.

What types of surgeries can be performed using robotic-assisted technology at ABMH?

At ABMH, robotic-assisted surgery is used for various specialties, including urology, gynecology, gastroenterology, oncology, and general surgery. Procedures such as prostatectomy, hysterectomy, colorectal surgery, and hernia repair benefit from robotic precision, reducing complications and recovery time.

Is robotic surgery safe?

Yes, robotic surgery is highly safe when performed by trained experts. It offers greater precision, smaller incisions, less blood loss, and faster recovery, reducing risks compared to traditional surgery. Our skilled surgeons at ABMH ensure patient safety at every step.
