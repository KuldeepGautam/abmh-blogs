Overview: Weight Loss Surgery Hospital in Pimpri Chinchwad

Bariatric surgery, also known as weight loss surgery, involves a variety of surgical procedures designed to help individuals struggling with obesity lose weight and improve their overall health. These surgeries work by altering the digestive system to either reduce the amount of food the stomach can hold or limit how much fat and calories are absorbed. Bariatric and Metabolic surgery is often recommended when traditional weight loss methods such as diet and exercise have not been successful, or when obesity is causing serious health complications.

Bariatric Surgery – Procedures

These are the surgical and endoscopic techniques used to reduce weight and improve metabolic health:

Surgical Procedures

  • Sleeve Gastrectomy
    • Removes ~75–80% of the stomach
    • Reduces food intake and hunger hormones
  • Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass
    • Creates a small stomach pouch
    • Bypasses a portion of the small intestine
    • Reduces calorie absorption and food intake
  • Mini Gastric Bypass (One Anastomosis Gastric Bypass)
    • Simpler version of traditional gastric bypass
    • Single connection to small intestine
  • Adjustable Gastric Banding
    • Band placed around upper stomach to restrict food intake
    • Adjustable and reversible
  • Biliopancreatic Diversion with Duodenal Switch (BPD/DS)
    • Combines sleeve gastrectomy with intestinal bypass
    • Significant weight loss with malabsorption
  • Revision Bariatric Surgery
    • Correction or conversion of previous weight loss surgeries
  • Non-Surgical / Endoscopic Procedures
  • Intragastric Balloon
    • Balloon placed in stomach via endoscopy
    • Occupies space to promote satiety
  • Endoscopic Sleeve Gastroplasty (ESG)
    1. Sutures used to reduce stomach size internally
    2. Minimally invasive with faster recovery

Frequently Ask Questions (FAQs)

1. What is bariatric surgery?

Bariatric surgery refers to a group of surgical procedures designed to help patients lose weight by altering their digestive systems. It’s often recommended when lifestyle changes alone are insufficient.

2. Who is a candidate for bariatric surgery?

You may be eligible if your BMI is 35 or higher and you have related health conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, or sleep apnea. Our bariatric surgeon in Pimpri Chinchwad conducts a thorough evaluation to determine suitability.
