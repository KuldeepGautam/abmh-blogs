Effective Home Remedies for Loose Motions: Simple Ways to Stop Them

Loose motions, or diarrhea, can be distressing and can affect your daily life. They often occur due to infections, food intolerances, or other underlying conditions. While over-the-counter medications are available, you can try treatment for loose motions at home with natural remedies and see if they work for you.

However, if you aren’t sure about this or have watery stools three or more times within a day’s span, visit a doctor at the earliest. 

Read on to know how to cure loose motions at home with simple, effective methods.

How to Cure Loose Motions at Home

When faced with loose motions, it’s crucial to address the condition promptly to avoid dehydration and other complications.

1. Stay Hydrated:

When you have loose motion (diarrhea), your body loses a lot of water and important salts. To feel better, you need to drink plenty of fluids like water, oral rehydration solutions (ORS), or clear soups. This helps replace the lost water and salts, keeping your body healthy.

2. Consume Yogurt:

Yogurt is good for your tummy because it has something called probiotics, which are tiny living organisms that help keep your stomach balanced and healthy. Eating yogurt can help stop loose motion. You can even add a little salt or honey to it to make it taste better.

3. Ginger Tea:

Ginger is a root that has special properties to reduce swelling and irritation in your stomach. Drinking ginger tea can help calm your stomach down and naturally stop loose motion.

4. Bananas:

Bananas contain a substance called pectin, which can absorb extra water in your intestines. This makes your stool firmer, which is helpful when you're dealing with loose motion. Bananas are also easy to digest and give your body important nutrients like potassium.

5. Cumin Water:

Cumin seeds are tiny seeds that help with digestion. Boil a teaspoon of cumin seeds in water, strain the seeds out, and drink the water. This can help you feel better when you have loose motion.

6. Avoid Certain Foods:

When you're dealing with loose motion, it's a good idea to avoid certain foods that might make things worse. Stay away from dairy products (except yogurt), spicy foods, and foods with a lot of fiber because they can irritate your stomach even more.

These remedies are simple ways to help your body recover when you have loose motion, but remember to see a doctor if the problem doesn't get better soon.

Also, ensure you do not use these on new borns as their food consumption and issues are quite different to adults.

When to See a Doctor

While home remedies for loose motion can be effective, it's important to know when to seek medical attention. You should consult a doctor if:

  • The symptoms persist for more than two days.
  • There is a presence of blood or mucus in the stool.
  • You experience severe abdominal pain or high fever.
  • Signs of dehydration like excessive thirst, dry mouth, or dizziness appear.

Consulting a specialist, especially from a reputable gastroenterology hospital, can help address any underlying issues and provide appropriate treatment.

FAQs- Home Remedies For Loose Motions

What is the fastest way to stop diarrhea?

Eating plain, bland foods, staying hydrated with water and clear fluids, and using over-the-counter anti-diarrheal medications can help you recover from mild diarrhea more quickly. Please do not consume medicines without speaking with a doctor.


What is the best drink for loose motion?

1. Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS):

ORS is specially designed to combat dehydration caused by diarrhea. It contains a balanced mix of salts and sugar, which helps your body absorb fluids more effectively. Health organizations, including the World Health Organization (WHO), endorse ORS as a key treatment for dehydration related to diarrhea. Research shows that ORS helps reduce dehydration and speeds up recovery.

2. Coconut Water:

Coconut water is a natural drink rich in electrolytes like potassium and magnesium, which are important for hydration. It's gentle on the stomach and helps replenish lost fluids. Studies indicate that coconut water can help maintain hydration during mild dehydration.

3. Plain Water:

Plain water is crucial for staying hydrated, but combining it with electrolyte-rich fluids like ORS can be more effective in managing dehydration during diarrhea.
