What is Mammography?

Mammography is a specialized X-ray test that enables doctors to examine breast tissue. It primarily detects early signs of breast cancer or other breast issues by producing clear breast images that reveal small lumps or changes not felt during a physical exam. Early detection enhances treatment effectiveness and patient outcomes.

Why is Mammography Done?

Doctors may recommend a mammography test for:

  • Breast cancer screening, especially for women above 40 years
  • Evaluating any lumps, pain, or discharge in the breast
  • Detecting changes in the breast not visible externally
  • Family history follow-up for breast cancer

How is the Test Done?

  • Stand in front of a mammography machine
  • The breast is gently placed between two plates
  • Plates press the breast briefly to capture a clear X-ray image
  • The process may cause mild discomfort but is not painful
  • The test typically lasts 15–20 minutes

Benefits of Mammography

  • Detects breast cancer early
  • Increases chances of successful treatment
  • Identifies tiny lumps not felt by hand
  • Safe and quick procedure

Preparation Before the Test

  • Avoid powder, deodorant, or lotion on the chest/underarms on test day
  • Wear comfortable clothing
  • Inform doctor if pregnant or breastfeeding

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is mammography painful?

No, it is not painful—only a few seconds of mild pressure are experienced.

2. At what age should women start mammography?

Screening is generally advised from age 40; earlier if there’s a family history of breast cancer.

3. How often should I get a mammography?

Typically every 1–2 years after age 40, depending on individual health conditions.

4. Is mammography safe?

Yes, the radiation amount is minimal and not harmful.

5. Can men undergo mammography?

Yes, though rare, men with breast problems may require this test.

6. How long does it take to get reports?

Reports are usually available the same day or next working day.
