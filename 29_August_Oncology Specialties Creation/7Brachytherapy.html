Treatment & Procedure: What is Brachytherapy?

Brachytherapy is a type of internal radiation therapy where radioactive material is placed inside or close to the tumor. It is commonly used for cancers such as:

  • Cervical cancer
  • Prostate cancer
  • Breast cancer
  • Head and neck cancers

Types of Brachytherapy Offered:

  • High-Dose Rate (HDR) Brachytherapy - Delivers radiation in short bursts over minutes.
  • Low-Dose Rate (LDR) Brachytherapy – Administers continuous radiation over hours or days.
  • Temporary or Permanent Implants - Depending on the type and stage of cancer.

At Aditya Birla Memorial Hospital, each patient’s treatment plan is created using advanced imaging and planning software to ensure maximum effectiveness with minimal discomfort.

How the Procedure Works:

  1. Diagnosis & Consultation: Patients meet with a Brachytherapy specialist doctor in Pune for a comprehensive evaluation.
  2. Treatment Planning: Advanced CT/MRI imaging helps plan the placement of radiation sources.
  3. Radiation Delivery: The radioactive material is placed in or near the tumor using minimally invasive techniques.
  4. Post-Treatment Monitoring: Patients are closely monitored and supported during their recovery process.

Brachytherapy is typically outpatient-based or may require short hospital stays, depending on the type and complexity of treatment.
