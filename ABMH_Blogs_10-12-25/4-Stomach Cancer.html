Stomach Cancer: Symptoms, Causes & Treatment

What is Stomach Cancer?

Stomach cancer, also called gastric cancer, happens when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in the stomach lining. These cells can form tumours in the stomach and may spread to nearby areas, such as the intestine or gallbladder, if left untreated.

Understanding what stomach cancer is helps people identify symptoms early and seek timely medical care. Although it develops slowly, early detection greatly improves treatment outcomes.

Recognising the Symptoms of Stomach Cancer

The symptoms of stomach cancer often appear similar to common digestive problems, which is why many people ignore them at first. Some early signs include:

  • Persistent indigestion or heartburn
  • Loss of appetite
  • Fatigue
  • Unexplained weight loss

As the condition progresses, abdominal discomfort becomes more noticeable. Some people experience:

  • A feeling of fullness even after small meals
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Blood in stool or vomit
  • Pain in the upper abdomen

Many symptoms overlap with those of abdominal tumours, making a proper diagnosis essential. If these symptoms continue for more than a few weeks, consulting a specialist is important.

Causes and Risk Factors of Stomach Cancer

The causes of stomach cancer are often linked to a combination of lifestyle, environmental, and genetic factors. Some known contributors include:

  • Long-term infection with H. pylori bacteria
  • Smoking
  • Diets high in salty and processed foods
  • Family history of gastric cancer
  • Existing stomach conditions, such as chronic gastritis
  • Obesity
  • Frequent acid reflux

Risk also increases with age, especially in individuals above 50.

Modern Approaches to Stomach Cancer Management

Stomach cancer treatment depends on the cancer’s stage and the patient’s overall health. Modern medical care offers several effective methods:

  1. Surgery: Surgical removal of part or all of the stomach is common in early and some advanced cases.
  2. Chemotherapy: Used to shrink tumours, prevent spread, or destroy remaining cancer cells after surgery.
  3. Radiation Therapy: Targets cancer cells with controlled radiation to reduce growth.
  4. Targeted Therapy: These medicines attack specific cancer cell changes while protecting healthy tissue.
  5. Immunotherapy: Helps the body’s immune system identify and fight cancer cells more effectively.

Doctors may also consider related conditions like gallbladder cancer or intestine cancer if symptoms overlap.

How Lifestyle and Diet Influence Stomach Cancer Risk

Daily habits play a major role in stomach health. Risk factors increase when a person:

  • Eats excessive processed or smoked foods
  • Consumes alcohol regularly
  • Smokes
  • Ignores long-term digestive issues
  • Lives a sedentary lifestyle

Healthy habits such as eating fresh fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and staying physically active can help lower the chances of stomach cancer. Limiting salt, pickles, and packaged foods is also beneficial.

When to Take Symptoms Seriously: Warning Signs of Abdominal Tumours

Symptoms like frequent bloating, stomach pain, or unexplained fatigue may not always indicate cancer, but persistent issues should never be ignored.

Warning signs of abdominal tumours include:

  • Sharp or constant pain in the upper abdomen
  • Sudden vomiting, especially with blood
  • Lump or swelling in the abdominal area
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Sudden weight loss without dieting

Recognising these early helps prevent advanced complications.

Living Well After a Stomach Cancer Diagnosis

A cancer diagnosis can feel overwhelming, but many people continue to live meaningful and healthy lives with the right medical support. Recovery often involves:

  • Regular follow-ups
  • Healthy eating plans
  • Emotional and mental support
  • Doctors recommend physical activity
  • Tracking any new symptoms
