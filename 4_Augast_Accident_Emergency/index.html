Emergency Care Treatment & Procedure We Provide

Common treatments offered in the Emergency and Trauma Care Department include:

  • Trauma Care: Management of injuries from accidents, fractures, and lacerations, including surgical intervention when necessary.
  • Cardiac Care: Immediate treatment for heart attacks, arrhythmias, and other cardiovascular emergencies with life-saving medications, CPR, or defibrillation.
  • Stroke Management: Rapid diagnosis and thrombolytic therapy or surgical intervention to minimize brain damage.
  • Respiratory Emergencies: Treatment for asthma attacks, severe difficulty in breathing, or respiratory failure, including oxygen therapy and ventilation support.
  • Severe Infections: Urgent management of life-threatening infections such as sepsis or pneumonia, with antibiotics and supportive care.
  • Paediatric Emergency Care: Addressing medical emergencies in children, including high fevers, respiratory issues, and trauma.
  • Poisoning or Overdose: Treatment for poisoning and overdoses, including detoxification and antidote administration.
  • Obstetric Emergencies: Care for pregnant women facing complications like bleeding, eclampsia, or premature labor.
  • Neurological Emergencies: Management of neurological conditions such as seizures, head trauma, or spinal injuries.
  • Fracture Management: Stabilization and setting of fractures and orthopedic trauma.
  • Wound Management: Suturing and dressing of deep cuts and abrasions to prevent infection and promote healing.

2. How do I know if I need to visit the emergency department?

You should visit the emergency department if you experience severe symptoms such as chest pain, difficulty breathing, heavy bleeding, severe trauma, signs of a stroke, or a sudden, severe headache. If you have a medical condition that worsens rapidly or poses an immediate risk to your health, seeking emergency care is essential.
