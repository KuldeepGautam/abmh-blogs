Why is this test done?

The 1,25 Dihydroxy Vitamin D Test measures the level of the active form of Vitamin D in your blood. This form, known as calcitriol, helps regulate calcium and phosphate in your body, which is essential for healthy bones and teeth.

Why Is This Test Done?

Doctors recommend this test to:

  • Check if your body is converting Vitamin D properly
  • Monitor kidney function (since kidneys help activate Vitamin D)
  • Investigate causes of calcium imbalances
  • Support diagnosis of conditions like parathyroid disorders, rickets, or osteoporosis

How to prepare for this test?

  • No fasting or special preparation is needed.
  • Continue regular medications unless advised otherwise by your doctor.

Who should get this test done?

You may need this test if you:

  • Show signs of vitamin D deficiency despite normal basic Vitamin D3 levels
  • Have kidney or parathyroid disorders
  • Experience symptoms like bone pain, fatigue, or frequent fractures
  • Are undergoing evaluation for metabolic bone diseases

When to consult a doctor after the test?

Consult your doctor if:

  • Your results are lower or higher than the normal range
  • You have underlying health conditions like kidney or parathyroid issues
  • You need help understanding your calcium or phosphate levels

1. Is this the same as a regular Vitamin D test?

No, this test measures the active form of Vitamin D (1,25-dihydroxy), while standard tests usually check the storage form (25-hydroxy Vitamin D).

2. Do I need this test if my basic Vitamin D levels are normal?

Possibly. If you have symptoms of deficiency or bone issues despite normal Vitamin D levels, your doctor may recommend this test.
