What is the Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT) Test?

The Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase Test in Pune is a blood test that helps evaluate liver function by measuring the level of the GGT enzyme. This enzyme plays a crucial role in breaking down toxins and drugs in the liver. High levels of GGT may indicate liver damage, bile duct problems, or excessive alcohol consumption.

Why is this Test Done?

Doctors recommend the Gamma Glutamyl Transferase Test in Pimpri Chinchwad to:

  • Detect liver diseases, including hepatitis and cirrhosis
  • Assess bile duct obstructions
  • Monitor alcohol-related liver damage
  • Evaluate possible medication effects on liver function

Parameters Covered:

This test primarily measures the Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT) enzyme levels in the blood.

How to Prepare for This Test?

To ensure accurate results:

  • Fasting: Avoid eating or drinking (except water) for at least 8 hours before the test.
  • Alcohol and Smoking: Refrain from consuming alcohol and smoking for at least 24 hours before the test, as these can elevate GGT levels.
  • Medications: Inform your doctor about any medications or supplements you're taking, as some drugs may affect the results.

Who Should Get This Test Done?

Individuals experiencing symptoms such as:

  • Fatigue
  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin or eyes)
  • Abdominal pain or swelling
  • Dark urine
  • Loss of appetite
  • Nausea or vomiting

When to Consult a Doctor After the Test?

Consult a healthcare professional if:

  • Your GGT levels are elevated
  • You have persistent symptoms of liver dysfunction
  • You have a history of liver disease or alcohol consumption
