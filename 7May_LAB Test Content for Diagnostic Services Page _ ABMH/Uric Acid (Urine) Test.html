What is this test?

The Uric Acid (Urine) Test measures the amount of uric acid excreted in your urine. Uric acid is a waste product formed when the body breaks down purines, which are found in certain foods and naturally in the body. The kidneys filter uric acid and remove it through urine. This test helps determine if your body is producing or eliminating too much uric acid, which can lead to conditions like kidney stones and gout.

Why is this test done?

This test helps in:

  • Diagnosing Kidney Stones – High uric acid levels in urine can increase the risk of kidney stone formation.
  • Evaluating Gout – This test helps determine if excess uric acid excretion is causing gout.
  • Assessing Kidney Function – The kidneys help remove uric acid; abnormal levels may indicate kidney problems.
  • Monitoring Uric Acid Disorders – Helps diagnose and monitor conditions like hyperuricemia.

A Uric Acid Test in Pune can help detect these issues early and allow for timely medical intervention.

What parameters are covered?

This test specifically measures the uric acid concentration in urine collected over a random or 24-hour period.

How to prepare for this test?

  • For a random urine test: No special preparation is needed.
  • For a 24-hour urine test:
    • Your doctor may advise avoiding high-purine foods (red meat, seafood) before the test.
    • Drink plenty of water unless instructed otherwise.
    • Follow the correct urine collection process as advised by the lab.

If you are booking a Uric Acid Test online in Chinchwad or PCMC, confirm with the lab whether a 24-hour urine sample is required.

Who should get this test done?

Consider taking this test if you:

  • Have a history of kidney stones.
  • Experience symptoms of gout, such as joint pain and swelling.
  • Have a family history of uric acid disorders.
  • Are undergoing treatment that may affect uric acid levels.

When to consult a doctor after the test?

See a doctor if your test results show abnormal uric acid levels, especially if you experience:

  • Severe joint pain (a symptom of gout)
  • Frequent kidney stones
  • Burning sensation during urination
  • Unexplained fatigue or weakness

Your doctor can provide recommendations on diet, lifestyle changes, or medications to manage your uric acid levels effectively.

1. What is the difference between a blood and urine uric acid test?

A blood uric acid test measures the level of uric acid in the bloodstream, while a urine uric acid test checks how much uric acid is excreted by the kidneys. Both tests may be recommended for a complete evaluation.

2. Can a high uric acid level in urine cause kidney stones?

Yes, excessive uric acid in urine can contribute to the formation of kidney stones, which may cause pain and urinary problems.

4. What factors can affect uric acid levels in urine?

  • Diet high in purines (red meat, shellfish, alcohol)
  • Dehydration
  • Kidney disease
  • Certain medications

5. How can I lower my uric acid levels?

  • Drink more water
  • Eat a balanced diet, limit purine-rich foods
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Follow doctor’s advice on medications
