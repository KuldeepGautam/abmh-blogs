What is this test?

The G6PD (Quantitative) test in Pimpri Pune is a blood test that measures the precise levels of the G6PD enzyme in red blood cells. This enzyme is essential for protecting red blood cells from damage. Low enzyme levels can cause hemolytic anemia, a condition where red blood cells break down too quickly. This test helps assess the severity of the deficiency and aids in proper management.

Why is this test done?

Doctors recommend booking a G6PD test near you if you:

  • Experience symptoms like fatigue, jaundice, or dark-colored urine
  • Have a family history of G6PD deficiency
  • Have suffered from anemia triggered by infections, medications, or certain foods (like fava beans)
  • Need screening before taking specific medications that could cause hemolysis in individuals with G6PD deficiency
  • Require detailed enzyme activity measurement for diagnosis and treatment planning

What parameters are covered?

  • G6PD enzyme activity level (measured quantitatively)
  • Hemoglobin concentration (if included in the test package)

How to Prepare for This Test?

  • No fasting or special preparation is required for the G6PD test in Pimpri Chinchwad
  • Inform your doctor if you have recently had a blood transfusion, as this may impact your test results

Who should get this test done?

Consider booking a G6PD test online in Chinchwad if you:

  • Have unexplained episodes of anemia
  • Are a newborn with a family history of G6PD deficiency
  • Need a confirmatory test after a qualitative G6PD screening
  • Belong to a high-risk group, such as people of Mediterranean, African, or Asian descent

When to consult a doctor after the test?

Visit a healthcare provider if:

  • Your G6PD levels are low, indicating a deficiency
  • You experience symptoms of hemolysis (fatigue, pale skin, jaundice, shortness of breath)
  • You need guidance on managing G6PD deficiency through diet and medication adjustments

1. What is the difference between a qualitative and quantitative G6PD test?

The qualitative G6PD test only detects whether a deficiency is present, while the quantitative test measures the exact enzyme activity level, helping determine the severity of the condition.

2. Can G6PD deficiency be cured?

No, G6PD deficiency is a genetic condition. However, avoiding triggers like certain medications, infections, and foods can help prevent complications.
