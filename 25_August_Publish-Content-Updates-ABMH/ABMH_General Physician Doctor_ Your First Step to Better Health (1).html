Let’s take a closer look at the crucial role general physicians play in modern healthcare, and why having one might be one of the smartest health decisions you can make.

Who is a General Physician?

A general physician is a medical doctor who provides primary healthcare services to individuals of all age groups. They diagnose and treat:

  • Common illnesses
  • Help manage chronic conditions
  • Offer preventive care, among other

Additionally, general physicians are also skilled in identifying when a condition needs specialist care and will refer you accordingly.

Why are General Physicians so Important today?

With more people living in cities, spending long hours at work, and leading stressful lives, new health challenges have emerged. Lifestyle diseases like hypertension, obesity, diabetes, and heart issues are rising fast. This is where general physicians are proving to be more valuable than ever.

5. They educate and empower patients

What Health Issues can a General Physician Treat?

General physicians deal with a broad range of medical problems, such as:

  • Common colds, flu, and infections
  • Stomach issues like acidity, constipation, or diarrhea
  • Skin problems like rashes, acne, or eczema
  • Respiratory conditions like asthma and bronchitis
  • Heart-related issues in early stages (like high blood pressure or chest discomfort)
  • Endocrine issues, including diabetes and thyroid problems
  • Mental health concerns like anxiety and depression

They also assist with minor injuries and post-surgery care, and can provide home care advice when needed.

When Should You Visit a General Physician?

You should visit a general physician when:

  • You feel unwell and don’t know what's causing it
  • You need a routine health checkup or vaccination
  • You're managing a long-term illness and need regular follow-ups
  • You're recovering from surgery or a hospital stay
  • You want guidance on diet, exercise, or stress
  • You need a medical referral for a specialist

Having a trusted family doctor can make these situations much less stressful.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. How is a general doctor different from a general physician?

A general doctor or general practitioner offers basic care for all age groups. A general physician typically treats adults and handles more complex, non-surgical conditions.

2. What does a general physician do?

A general physician specialises in diagnosing and managing serious or complex medical issues in adults, offering ongoing care until the condition is stable or resolved.

3. Can general physicians perform surgery?

No, general physicians do not perform surgeries. General or specialised surgeons handle surgical procedures.
