7 Tips for Relieving Joint Pain in Winter

Eating well, staying hydrated, exercising, getting some sunlight, and using knee support gears can all help reduce joint pain in the winter.

-------------------------

For many people, especially those with arthritis or similar conditions, winter can make joint pain worse. The cold temperatures can cause your muscles to lose heat and tighten, increasing instances of joint stiffness, aches, and pains.

If you often experience joint pain and stiffness in the winter, here are 7 easy tips to relieve joint pain.

Get Relief From Joint Pain in Winter with These 7 Tips

  • Tip 1: Heat Therapy

    Heat therapy is a great way to reduce joint pain and stiffness and improve mobility. It relaxes the muscles and increases circulation to the affected area, which helps ease discomfort and tightness.

    One of the easiest and most preferred heat therapy methods is using hot packs, as you can easily apply them at home. Heat therapy can also be combined with other methods to further relieve joint pain in winter.

  • Tip 2: Anti-Inflammatory Foods

    Eating foods like avocado, nuts, and seeds can reduce inflammation. Increasing an intake of anti-inflammatory foods can help improve physical characteristics, stress, and pain. However, please check with your doctor if you have any food allergies.

    Here are some great anti-inflammatory foods you can have to reduce joint pain in winter:

    1. Turmeric
    2. Garlic
    3. Red onions
    4. Green tea
    5. Grapefruit
    6. Berries
    7. Probiotic foods like yoghurt
    8. Leafy greens

  • Tip 3: Stay Active

    Exercise is key for managing joint pain, as it prevents stiffness and strengthens muscles, which helps support your joints. Even though it’s harder to stay active in winter, you can still find ways to move indoors. Switch to low-impact activities like using an elliptical trainer or brisk walking, which are great ways to stay active in winter. Just remember to warm up and stretch before exercising.

  • Tip 4: Take Vitamin D Supplements

    Vitamin D deficiency can make joint pain worse. Since we get less sunlight in the winter, you might have lower levels of this vitamin. Talk to your doctor about whether you should take vitamin D supplements.

  • Tip 5: Stay Hydrated and Have a Balanced Diet

    In winter, it’s easy to forget to drink enough water since you’re not thirsty or sweating. However, the cold weather can still dehydrate you, which can make the pain feel worse. Remember to drink plenty of fluids.

    In addition to staying hydrated, having a balanced diet also helps reduce joint pain in winter. Add food items that contain minerals like calcium, vitamin D, and more. Avoid carbohydrates, processed foods, and excessive amounts of salt and vinegar. You can consult a dietician to help you maintain a healthy and balanced diet.

  • Tip 6: Get a Massage

    Massages can be a great way to manage pain. A trained massage therapist can help relieve sore muscles and improve blood flow to the joints, which can be especially helpful in reducing joint pain during the winter. This is one of the most effective methods for easing discomfort and keeping joints healthy. Speak with a physiotherapist to understand the type of massages you can take.

  • Tip 7: Use Medication Wisely

    While lifestyle changes are great for managing joint pain in winter, there may be days when you need extra relief. Before taking any pain medications like ibuprofen or acetaminophen, check with your doctor to make sure they’re safe for you, especially if you have conditions like stomach ulcers or kidney issues.

Reasons for Joint Pain in Winter

Besides the colder temperatures and stiff joints, other factors contribute to joint pain in winter. Here we have listed a few reasons why the joint pain in winter remedies isn’t working for you:

  1. Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

    Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) is a long-term condition. It is an inflammatory condition in which small joints of the hands and wrists usually swell. In some people, RA can damage body systems, such as eyes, lungs, skin, and heart and blood vessels.

    Common symptoms include:

    • Fatigue
    • Tiredness
    • Weight loss
    • Joint stiffness, and more

  2. Osteoarthritis

    Osteoarthritis is the most common form of arthritis, affecting millions worldwide. It happens when the cartilage that cushions the joints wears down, causing the bones to rub against each other, leading to pain and swelling. While older adults are more likely to develop it, people of all ages can be affected. It often impacts the knees, hips, lower back, neck, fingers, thumbs, and big toes.

  3. Hypothyroidism

    Hypothyroidism happens when the thyroid gland doesn’t make enough hormones for the body. On the flip side, hyperthyroidism occurs when the thyroid makes too many hormones. These thyroid issues are more common in women, especially older women, and they can cause joint pain.
